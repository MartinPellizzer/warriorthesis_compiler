
Gossiping is one of the most common forms of workplace bullying behavior. It can be hurtful, damaging and difficult to confront. Gossiping can be a way of spreading false information, rumors, or exaggerations about a person or group. This type of bullying can be especially damaging because it is often done behind the scenes and can be hard to prove. The effects of gossiping can be long lasting, and can lead to feelings of isolation, fear, and mistrust. It is important to remember that gossiping is not acceptable in any workplace and should be addressed immediately. If you feel like you are being gossiped about, it is important to reach out for support from trusted colleagues or friends. It is also important to remember that you are not alone and there are resources available to help you.
