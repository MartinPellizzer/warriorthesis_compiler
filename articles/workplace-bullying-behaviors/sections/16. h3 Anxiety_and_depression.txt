
Anxiety and depression are very real issues that can be caused by workplace bullying behaviors. It can be difficult to know how to cope with these feelings, especially when they are caused by someone else's actions. It's important to remember that you are not alone in this experience, and that there are steps you can take to help manage your anxiety and depression. Start by talking to someone you trust, such as a family member or friend. You can also reach out to a mental health professional for additional support. Taking the time to practice self-care can also be very helpful in managing your anxiety and depression. This could include activities such as exercise, meditation, or journaling. Lastly, if the workplace bullying behaviors continue, it may be beneficial to talk to a supervisor or HR representative. Remember that you are not alone and that there are resources available to help you cope with these difficult emotions.
